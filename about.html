80 Years of Innovation

Eight decades of milestones that shaped PCB drilling, from the first European drilling machine to X-Ray inspection.

1943

Schmoll Foundation

Foundation of Schmoll Maschinen by Heinz Schmoll in Kronberg, Germany.

1962

First PCB Drilling Machine

The first PCB drilling machine in Europe, a defining milestone for the electronics manufacturing industry.

1985

Twin Concept

The Twin concept introduced two spindles working simultaneously, significantly increasing productivity and drilling precision.

1992

Contact Bit Detection

CBD detects broken drill bits in real time by monitoring the electrical contact between bit and panel surface, preventing defects.

1993

Automation

Automated loading and unloading, robotic arms and intelligent control systems raise efficiency, reduce manual labor and minimize errors.

1993

Thomas Kunz

At 33, Thomas Kunz led Schmoll Maschinen out of insolvency. Over 32 years his leadership transformed Schmoll into a PCB industry leader.

1999

Linear Motor

Linear motors deliver high speed, exceptional positioning accuracy and excellent dynamic behavior in PCB drilling machines.

2004

MXY-CCD Concept

High-end drilling combined with CCD-controlled reference systems enables precise back drilling and accurate positioning for complex designs.

2006

300K Sub-Spindle

A high-speed sub-spindle for small-diameter holes, running up to 300,000 RPM with exceptional precision and efficiency.

2010

Direct Imaging

Direct Imaging technology revolutionizes PCB production by eliminating the need for traditional photomasks.

2015

Laser Drilling

Laser drilling creates microvias and through-holes with unparalleled precision and speed, reaching extremely small, intricate holes.

2023

X-Ray Inspection

X-Ray systems enable non-destructive testing and alignment, allowing detailed examination of inner layers and solder joints.
